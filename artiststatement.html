Artist Statement

My work dares to explore manipulating reality, which questions how we visualize our world and the “reality” that my work reflects. I envision my work as a mirror of the real world and not just another false reality.

​

As a growing artist, I am trying to find what works in my pieces and what doesn’t. My work is very experimental, and technology has given me a platform that allows for experimentation and trial and error. Before, I thought that human emotion was the sole driving force of my work. However, there is a different emotion when one sees a different reflection of reality.

In many of my works, I’ve played with time (fast-forwarding or slow motion) and with color (emphasizing an element’s color or changing the color of something) to play with the audience’s vision and interpretation of my work and the world they know. An example is one of my digital photographs, Fenced. I played with heavy black in the background, a vibrant pink flower in the center, and gray rectangles that act as windows to see the flower. I wanted to experiment with how we visualize the content by using shapes and color to dictate what the viewer sees.

Technology is a rising factor of everyday life. I want my audience to see how I have taken advantage of what technology has given me. As an artist who has worked with both old media and new media, I want to show how my art has transitioned from the old to the new.
