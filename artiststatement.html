Artist Statement

My work dares to explore associating art with human emotions, especially the ones that are difficult to express. I envision my work as an extension of emotional expression.

​

Human emotion has been a driving force for much of my work. The emotions that run through me manifest themselves in the photographs and videos I have documented. Emotion is a valuable part of personality and being able to add a feeling to a piece gives my art personal meaning.

In one of my works, Fenced, I explored the idea of beauty tucked away by darkness. I made associations with the feeling of happiness as beauty and the feeling of anxiety as darkness. Using objects, distorting or strengthening their color, and the use of shapes to hide or highlight gives a hint of certain emotions. Putting those two emotions and associations together, I wanted to highlight the struggles of being anxious and worried while trying to find happiness.

I want my audience to generate emotions with my work, whether it is similar feeling or not. Being able to bring about an emotion from a work is an aspect that I look for when I am perusing through other works. Putting myself in someone else’s shoes, seeing their world in their eyes, and trying to feel what they feel is something I strive to do as an artist and as a member of a growing society where we sometimes don’t understand the circumstances others are going through.
